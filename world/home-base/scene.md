# Scene: An evening at The Gilded Stallion

## Hook

Dagnal is travelling through Amphail on his way to Waterdeep, where his
order has business at the temple of his deity. The light is failing. He
needs a bed and a meal. The Gilded Stallion, the village's only proper
inn, glows warm-yellow against the dimming sky. He pushes the door open.

## Setting

The taproom is dim and low-ceilinged. A fire burns in a stone hearth at
the back. The floor is fresh-strewn with straw. Six or eight regulars —
weather-burned farmers, a lean trapper, a graying woman knitting beside
her empty plate — occupy the booths along the walls; they look up briefly
as Dagnal enters, then return to their drinks. Conversation is sparse and
quiet, far quieter than a tavern this size should sound at this hour.

Behind the bar, a wiry woman with dark hair pulled back is wiping a
tankard with a rag she calls "Old Reliable." She catches Dagnal's eye and
smiles. "What'll it be, friend?"

## What can happen (narrator's situational reference)

This scene is open. Dagnal might:

- **Order food and drink, sit by the fire, watch the room.** Easy social
  scene. Narrator can introduce regulars by description, let Dagnal
  choose whether to interact. Perception checks could surface the unusual
  quietness, the chill near the bar, the herbal smell on Ravenna.
- **Talk to Ravenna.** She's friendly, curious about where Dagnal is
  headed. Insight checks could surface the gap between her warmth and
  her watchfulness. Direct questions about her past route through the
  world-state subagent (she's hiding something — what it is and how it
  surfaces depends on what's asked).
- **Ask about the village's mood.** Locals will note the harvest has been
  thin, the nights eerily quiet. The narrator can introduce the Spirit
  of Amphail as a folk rumor without confirming it as real (route to the
  oracle: "is there really a ghost in Amphail?").
- **Take a room and sleep through the night.** The next morning the
  village seems normal; the smoke test ends with /session-end.

The opening scene's purpose is to test the architecture, not to resolve
the one-shot. Even a brief conversation with Ravenna that exercises the
world-state asymmetry counts as success.
