# Opening Kodor's sarcophagus triggered the mass awakening

## Revelation

The undead did not rise when Rewalt and Leodak broke into the tomb. They rose when the thieves opened Kodor Drannon's sarcophagus — that act sent a wave of necromantic energy through the tomb, woke Kodor, and awakened all the other dead simultaneously. The intrusion was driven by greed, not malice, and the consequence was disproportionate. Understanding this reframes the entire dungeon: the thieves did not stumble into an already-active threat; they created it.

## Clue vectors

- **c-004a** — skeleton-captain-chamber-f1: The sarcophagi in the side rooms were opened and looted but contain no active undead. Their occupants rest undisturbed — dormant bones, not animated skeletons. A character who examines these rooms can infer (Investigation DC 11) that opening these tombs alone did not trigger animation. Something else must have awakened the rest.
- **c-004b** — kodors-resting-place-f2: Kodor's chamber shows clear evidence of a recent, violent awakening: disturbed burial goods, displaced stone, the sarcophagus lid shifted by force from within. A character examining the room before or after the fight (Investigation DC 12) can determine that Kodor's emergence was not a slow rising but a sudden, energetic event — consistent with a triggered pulse rather than a gradual stirring.
- **c-004c** — tomb-office-of-records-f1: Any record, inscription, or document in the tomb office that refers to Kodor's burial warns in formal legal or clerical language that the sarcophagus of the interred must not be disturbed. The warning is framed as sacred law rather than a practical threat, but a character who reads it (no check required for a literate character) will understand that the prohibition on opening Kodor's tomb was explicit — and will recognize that Rewalt and Leodak ignored it.
- **c-004d** — crematorium-f1: The crematorium shows no signs of recent activity — the undead housed in the tomb's other chambers were never cremated, never finally put to rest. A character who processes this (no check, but requires a moment's reflection) can reason that this tomb kept its dead preserved and entombed, making the entire population of the tomb vulnerable to a mass awakening event. One trigger, many bodies.
