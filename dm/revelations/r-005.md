# Kodor received exactly what Myrkul promised

## Revelation

Kodor Drannon was in life a wizard and a follower of Myrkul, god of the dead. The book on the altar in his chamber records Myrkul's doctrine: spread fear of Myrkul in exchange for great power and eternal life. Kodor has received that eternal life literally — he now exists as an undead in the very tomb built to house his corpse. The horror is that the bargain was kept. His present state is not a curse or a corruption; it is the promised reward, fulfilled.

## Clue vectors

- **c-005a** — kodors-resting-place-f2: The book on the altar in Kodor's chamber records Myrkul's doctrine in explicit terms: serve Myrkul, spread fear of him, and receive great power and eternal life. A character who reads it (no check required for a literate character; the text is meant to be found) can draw the direct line between the promise on the page and the undead creature who just tried to kill them. Kodor IS the eternal life the book describes.
- **c-005b** — tomb-office-of-records-f1: The tomb records identify Kodor Drannon as the primary interred and include information about his life — his title, his practice, and (if the records are thorough) religious affiliations or patron dedications. A character who reads through the records (Investigation DC 10) can find references to Myrkul or to necromantic study, establishing Kodor's allegiance before the party ever reaches his chamber.
- **c-005c** — narrow-passage-f1: The tomb's architecture between F1 and F2 shifts in character — the construction changes in ways (carvings, iconography, material choice) that a character with religious knowledge (Religion DC 12) can identify as consistent with Myrkulite burial traditions: the emphasis on preservation over cremation, the warding inscriptions, the deliberate entombment of the dead rather than dispersal of remains. The tomb was built for Myrkul's faithful, which means Kodor's presence here is not incidental.
