# The halfling body in the staircase is Leodak

## Revelation

The halfling body found at the base of the staircase to Floor 2, marked by undead claw and bite wounds, is Leodak — the companion Rewalt mentions when explaining why he is alone. Rewalt's account describes a male halfling who didn't make it out from the second floor. The body matches. This correspondence makes Rewalt's cover story more plausible and is the evidence he would implicitly rely on if pressed. The revelation cuts both ways: it confirms Rewalt's companion genuinely died here, even as Rewalt uses that death to excuse his own culpability.

## Clue vectors

- **c-003a** — entrance-to-caverns-f2: The halfling body lies at the base of the Floor 2 staircase. A character who examines it can determine the wounds are consistent with undead attack (Investigation or Medicine DC 10) and note the victim's race and approximate age. At this point it is simply a corpse — the revelation requires connecting it to Rewalt's account.
- **c-003b** — cemetery-exterior: Rewalt, when pressed about how he came to be alone in the tomb, describes his companion — a male halfling named Leodak — who was separated from him on the second floor and never came back. If the party later finds the body, the details (halfling, male, second floor, undead wounds) align precisely. A player who remembers Rewalt's description will recognize the match without a roll.
- **c-003c** — skeleton-captain-chamber-f1: The side rooms off the Skeleton Captain Chamber show two distinct sets of interference: the sarcophagi were opened by living hands (deliberate looting), and drag marks or disturbed dust near the floor suggest two people moved through here in a hurry. A character who looks carefully (Investigation DC 13) can infer two intruders, supporting Rewalt's account of a pair — and making the halfling body's identity more inferrable once found.
