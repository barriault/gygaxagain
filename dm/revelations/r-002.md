# Rewalt lied — he never turned himself in

## Revelation

Rewalt Mason promised the party he would surrender himself to the guards at the tomb entrance when they returned to the surface. He did not. Instead he gave the guards a false account, claiming the undead dragged him into the tomb against his will. The party receives no reward for capturing or exposing him; the lie lands as a narrative consequence when they surface after defeating Kodor.

## Clue vectors

- **c-002a** — cemetery-exterior: When the party first encounters Rewalt at the entrance or nearby, a Wisdom (Insight) check against his manner of speech — especially his quick rehearsed offer to "make things right" and his careful omission of how he entered the tomb — surfaces the sense that he is managing his story rather than telling it. He is too ready with the self-accusation to be fully believed.
- **c-002b** — tomb-entrance-f1: If the party speaks with the guards at the tomb entrance before descending, the guards can describe the two men who were seen near the tomb before the undead became active. Their physical descriptions match Rewalt and Leodak precisely — placing both men here deliberately, not as victims of the dead.
- **c-002c** — skeleton-captain-chamber-f1: The opened sarcophagi in the side rooms off the Skeleton Captain Chamber show clear signs of deliberate looting — lids removed neatly, contents disturbed by searching hands, not by undead rampage. A character who examines the evidence (Investigation DC 12) can conclude the tombs were opened by living hands. This material evidence makes Rewalt's "dragged in by the undead" story implausible before he even tells it.
- **c-002d** — kodors-resting-place-f2: When the party defeats Kodor and moves to leave the tomb, the weight of what they accomplished — stopping the source of the undead awakening — sets up the expectation of a reward. Returning to the surface and finding no recognition from the guards (who relay Rewalt's version of events) lands the betrayal as a concrete narrative beat. The absence of acknowledgment is the final clue that the story has already been told without them.
