## Conditional connections

- **Tomb Office of Records F1 — door conditional:** The door is locked from the inside. Rewalt Mason will not open it until the party informs him the skeletons in the Tomb Entrance have been defeated. The party must defeat the 2 skeletons in tomb-entrance-f1 before Rewalt will unlock the door.

- **Skeleton Captain Chamber F1 — sarcophagus side rooms:** The doors left and ahead lead to small side rooms containing opened, emptied sarcophagi. These are dead-end nodes with no further exits and no encounters. They can be explored but contain nothing of value.

- **Floor 2 visibility:** All of Floor 2 is unlit. Without a light source (torches, lantern, etc.) or darkvision, the party cannot navigate Floor 2 safely.

## Clue dependencies

- **Leodak's body (entrance-to-caverns-f2):** The Halfling body at the bottom of the staircase is Leodak, Rewalt's companion. The party can identify him as Halfling. If the party spoke with Rewalt and learned about "Leodak," discovering the body confirms his fate. This clue is available without having spoken to Rewalt but is most significant after doing so.

- **Rewalt's intelligence (tomb-office-of-records-f1):** Rewalt warns about Floor 2 traps and explains the sarcophagus-opening incident. This gives the party advance context before descending. The party can descend without this context but risks the traps without forewarning.

- **Myrkul's altar and book (kodors-resting-place-f2):** Inspecting the altar symbol and the book provides lore context for Kodor's motivation and the Myrkul cult connection. This is discoverable only after defeating Kodor (or approaching the altar during combat).

- **Impaled skeleton warning (left-tunnel-f2):** The skeleton already killed by the spear trap is a visible environmental cue that spear traps exist here, but the crossbow trap on the chest is separate and still active regardless.
