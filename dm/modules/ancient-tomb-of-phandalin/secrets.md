## Twists & reveals

- **Rewalt's betrayal:** After being rescued, Rewalt promises to turn himself in to the guards at the tomb entrance. He does not. Instead, he later tells the guards he was dragged into the tomb by the undead. The party learns this in the "Afterward" resolution — when they return to the surface after defeating Kodor, they are told Rewalt did not turn himself in and gave the guards a false story. The party is not rewarded for capturing or exposing Rewalt; this is a narrative consequence only.

- **Leodak's death confirms Rewalt's story:** The Halfling body at the base of the Floor 2 staircase is Leodak, Rewalt's companion. His body showing claw and bite marks from undead corroborates Rewalt's account that they were separated on the second floor and Leodak didn't make it out. This is implicit in the module — the body's species and description match Rewalt's description of Leodak (male Halfling).

- **Kodor's sarcophagus opening triggered the mass undead awakening:** It was not the initial intrusion by the thieves but specifically the opening of Kodor Drannon's sarcophagus that sent a wave of energy through the tomb, woke Kodor, and awakened all the other dead. The thieves triggered this through greed, not malice.

## Hidden NPC identities & motives

- **Kodor Drannon:** A powerful wizard in life, now undead, who was a follower of Myrkul (god of the dead). His motivation is implicit rather than spoken: the book on the altar reveals Myrkul's doctrine — to spread fear of Myrkul in exchange for great power and eternal life. Kodor has literally received eternal life in undead form. He does not speak or negotiate; he attacks immediately upon the party entering his chamber.

- **Rewalt Mason:** Presents himself as a hapless victim who wants to make things right. His actual character: he is willing to lie to avoid consequences, blaming the undead for his presence in the tomb rather than his own deliberate break-in. He is not dangerous to the party but is not trustworthy.

## Hidden locations / passages

- **Sarcophagus side rooms (Skeleton Captain Chamber F1):** The doors to the left and ahead in the Skeleton Captain Chamber lead to small rooms containing already-opened sarcophagi. These are effectively dead-ends with nothing of value — their only narrative purpose is to show evidence of the theft (opened, looted tombs) that triggered Kodor's awakening.

- **The chest trap (Left Tunnel F2):** The crossbow trap on the wooden chest is distinct from the already-sprung spear wall trap. The impaled skeleton is a warning that traps exist, but the chest trap is independent and still active. A player who assumes all traps were already sprung by the skeleton will be surprised.

## DM-only context

- The module awards quest completion XP (200 XP per character) and GP (15 GP per character) upon returning to the surface and reporting Kodor's defeat. This is separate from encounter XP accumulated during the dungeon.
- Trap avoidance/disarming also awards 15 XP per character per trap.
- During the quest the party is expected to advance from 1st to 2nd level. The module is calibrated for four or five 1st level characters.
- The module can be relocated to any human settlement — Phandalin is a default, not a requirement.
- Two new monster stat blocks (Skeleton Captain and Kodor Drannon) are introduced in the appendix (see below).

## Custom stat blocks

### Skeleton Captain
- **Type:** Medium undead, lawful evil
- **Armor Class:** 14 (studded leather armor)
- **Hit Points:** 16 (3d8 + 3)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.
- **STR** 11 (+0) | **DEX** 14 (+2) | **CON** 12 (+1) | **INT** 6 (-2) | **WIS** 8 (-1) | **CHA** 5 (-3)
- **Damage Vulnerabilities:** Bludgeoning
- **Damage Immunities:** Poison
- **Condition Immunities:** Poisoned
- **Senses:** Passive Perception 9, darkvision 60 ft.
- **Languages:** Understands languages it knew in life but can't speak
- **Challenge:** 1/2 (100 XP)
- **Actions:**
  - **Multiattack.** The skeleton captain makes two melee attacks.
  - **Shortsword.** Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage.
