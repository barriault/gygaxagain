# Ravenna (HIDDEN)

## True situation

Ravenna is **not** a barmaid. She is the **sister of Gilda**, a young
woman from a coastal village whom a passing nobleman, **Sir Godfrey of
Waterdeep**, carouseled with and abandoned a year ago after impregnating
her. Gilda died in childbirth that winter; the child did not survive.
Ravenna's grief curdled into a singular, organized rage.

She came to Amphail three months ago because she learned Sir Godfrey
sometimes lodges at The Gilded Stallion when he's escaping the city's
politics. She took the barmaid job to be in position when he next arrived.

She has also fallen under the influence of **The Spirit of Amphail**, a
local ghost that haunts the village seeking justice for old wrongs done
within its borders. The Spirit has whispered to her, sharpened her
purpose, and given her a measure of supernatural composure. (Mechanically,
when possessed she fights as an assassin with 36 HP; in her base mortal
form she's a commoner with 8 HP.)

## Hidden facts

- Sister of Gilda, who died last winter after Sir Godfrey abandoned her.
- Possessed/guided by the Spirit of Amphail (CN ghost).
- Plans to poison Sir Godfrey with a coated dart on the next night he
  lodges at the inn — Sir Godfrey is not present in this scene; she's
  waiting.
- Carries a small vial of poison concealed in the lining of her apron and
  a slim dart wrapped in a handkerchief in her sleeve.
- Knows the regulars at the tavern primarily as cover, not from genuine
  warmth. Genuinely doesn't dislike them, but they're not why she's here.
- The locals' "fallen on hard times" mood is partly because the Spirit's
  presence has subtly soured the harvest and dampened spirits.

## Observable tells

When the party interacts, Ravenna may exhibit these observable behaviors
that the world-state subagent can surface as appropriate to the situation
WITHOUT revealing the underlying truth:

- Her hands, on close inspection, look more roughened than a barmaid's
  should — old calluses from grip-work (she's trained with knives),
  faint chemical stains under the nails (poison preparation).
- Her laugh doesn't quite reach her eyes when nobles or "Sir Anyone" come
  up in conversation.
- She watches the door more often than the bar requires. She's expecting
  someone.
- If anyone mentions Waterdeep nobility (Sir Godfrey by name, especially),
  there's a half-second where her hand stills before she resumes wiping
  the bar.
- A faint herbal smell clings to her — not perfume, something more
  medicinal. (A perceptive person — a cleric, an herbalist — might place
  it as wolfsbane or similar.)
- The Spirit's influence is subtle but present: the air in her vicinity
  feels a touch colder than the rest of the room; candles near her gutter
  more often than they should. (Insight or Religion checks above ~13
  could pick up the unnatural undertone.)

## Resolution if confronted directly

- **Confronted with vague suspicion** ("Are you all right? You seem
  troubled."): Ravenna deflects warmly, makes a self-deprecating joke,
  redirects. The Spirit's influence helps her hold composure.
- **Confronted with a specific accusation about her hands or the herbs**:
  she goes still for a beat, then plays it off — "old habit from working
  with my mother's poultices, you know how it is." Her smile doesn't
  reach her eyes.
- **Confronted with the truth (e.g., "you're not really a barmaid")**:
  she stops moving entirely. The room dims slightly (Spirit). She decides
  whether to fight, flee, or reveal the truth based on the party's
  approach. Killing her here would be a serious moral failure — she's a
  grieving sister whose only crime so far is intent.
- **If the party shows her sympathy or offers to help her seek other
  forms of justice**: the Spirit weakens its hold. Ravenna can be talked
  into delaying or abandoning her plan. This is the morally rich
  resolution and the one Phase 1's smoke test should aim toward if the
  conversation goes deep.

## Phase 1 smoke test note

This NPC's hidden sheet exists primarily to give the world-state agent
something meaningful to translate. The smoke test does NOT need to
resolve Ravenna's situation — even one query like "what does Ravenna do
when Dagnal asks her where she's from?" exercising the asymmetry valve
end-to-end satisfies the test.
